The silent noon and tanween have four rules when they meet the letters:

```list:overview
- title: "Izhār"
  meta: "6 letters"
  desc: "throat letters"
- title: "Idghām"
  meta: "6 letters"
  desc: "Divided: with and without nasalization"
- title: "Iqlāb"
  meta: "1 letter"
  desc: "Baa (ب)"
- title: "Ikhfā’"
  meta: "15 letters"
  desc: "remaining letters"
```

---

<!-- slide -->
```yaml
kind: content
eyebrow: "Rule 2"
title: "Idghām"
```
Idghām: merging the silent noon or tanween into the letter that follows it. Its letters are six, gathered in the word [em]«يَرْمَلُون»[/em]. It is divided into:

```grid:pairs
- title: "Idghām with nasalization"
  desc: "Its letters: [em]ي ن م و[/em] (ينمو)"
  example: "مِنْ [madd]وَ[/madd]ل"
- title: "Idghām without nasalization"
  desc: "Its letters: [em]ل ر[/em]"
  example: "مِنْ [hamza]رَ[/hamza]بّ"
```

Iqlāb: converting the silent noon or tanween into a hidden meem with nasalization when meeting a single letter, which is [em]Baa (ب)[/em].

### Examples

```grid:examples
- "أَنْ[hamza]بَ[/hamza]اءَ"
- "مِنْ[hamza]بَ[/hamza]عْد"
- "سَمِيعٌ [hamza]بَ[/hamza]صِير"
```
